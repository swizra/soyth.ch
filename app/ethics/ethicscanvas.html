Ethics Canvas: Social Media Algorithms & Democracy

Stakeholders

  • Social media users (billions)
  • Democratic institutions
  • Tech companies
  • Content creators
  • Vulnerable communities

Values at Stake

  • Democracy & informed citizenship
  • Freedom of speech
  • Truth & transparency
  • Privacy & autonomy
  • Fairness & equality

Potential Harms

  • Spread of misinformation
  • Political manipulation
  • Societal polarization
  • Erosion of institutional trust
  • Real-world violence

Potential Benefits

  • Increased political participation
  • Community building
  • Personalized information
  • Democratic discourse
  • Youth engagement

Tensions & Trade-offs

  • Free speech vs. harm prevention
  • Personalization vs. filter bubbles
  • Engagement vs. truth
  • Profit vs. democratic responsibility
  • Innovation vs. regulation

Responsibilities

  • Platforms: Transparency & ethics
  • Regulators: Clear laws & oversight
  • Users: Media literacy
  • Designers: Value-sensitive design

Action Items

  • Implement algorithm transparency
  • Strengthen content moderation policies
  • Conduct regular ethical audits
  • Establish multi-stakeholder governance
  • Develop public media literacy programs
  • Create enforceable accountability standards
