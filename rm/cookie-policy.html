Cookie Policy

Last Updated: July 22, 2025

At Soyth, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring transparency about how we use cookies and similar technologies on our website, https://soyth.ch. This Cookie Policy explains what cookies are, how we use them, and how you can manage your preferences in compliance with Swiss data protection laws, the European Union’s General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and other applicable regulations in regions where our services are accessed.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your actions and preferences, enhance site functionality, and provide insights into how the site is used. Other technologies, such as local storage, may also be used for similar purposes.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

We use the following categories of cookies on our website:

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly, such as enabling navigation, accessing secure areas, and maintaining your preferences (e.g., language or theme settings). They are always enabled and cannot be disabled as they are critical to the site’s operation.
  • Analytics Cookies: We use Google Analytics to collect anonymized data about how visitors interact with our website, such as which pages are visited and how long users stay on the site. This helps us improve our services and user experience. These cookies are optional and only enabled with your consent.
  • Marketing Cookies: These cookies help us understand user behavior to deliver relevant marketing content. They are optional and only enabled with your consent.

3. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Site Functionality: Essential cookies ensure that features like the language selector, theme toggle, and cookie preference management work correctly.
  • Performance and Analytics: With your consent, we use Google Analytics (ID: G-T9JP67T6DX) to collect anonymized data, such as page views, session duration, and bounce rates. This helps us optimize the website and improve content delivery.
  • Marketing: With your consent, marketing cookies may be used to track user preferences and behavior to tailor promotional content.

We do not use cookies to collect personal data beyond what is necessary for the website’s functionality or analytics purposes (with your consent). Any data collected via Google Analytics is anonymized by default to protect your privacy.

4. Legal Basis for Using Cookies

In accordance with Swiss data protection laws and the EU GDPR, we rely on the following legal bases for processing cookies:

  • Legitimate Interest: Essential cookies are used based on our legitimate interest in providing a functional and secure website.
  • Consent: Analytics and marketing cookies are only used with your explicit consent, which you can provide or withdraw at any time via our cookie banner or the "Manage Cookies" link in the footer.

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

When you first visit our website, a cookie banner will appear, allowing you to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customize your preferences. You can manage your cookie settings at any time by clicking the "Manage Cookies" link in the footer or the cookie banner.

To customize your preferences:

  • Essential Cookies: Always enabled, as they are required for the website to function.
  • Analytics Cookies: Enable or disable Google Analytics tracking.
  • Marketing Cookies: Enable or disable cookies used for marketing purposes.

Your preferences are stored in your browser’s local storage and can be updated at any time. You can also clear cookies via your browser settings, but this may reset your preferences and affect site functionality.

6. Third-Party Cookies

We use Google Analytics, a third-party service, to collect anonymized usage data. Google Analytics may set cookies on your device if you consent to analytics cookies. These cookies are subject to Google’s privacy policies, which you can review at Google’s Privacy Policy.

No other third-party cookies are used unless explicitly stated and consented to by you.

7. Data Retention

Essential cookies are stored only for as long as necessary to provide site functionality, typically until the end of your browsing session or as needed for persistent settings (e.g., theme or language preferences). Analytics and marketing cookies, if enabled, are retained for up to 24 months, as configured in Google Analytics. You can delete cookies at any time via your browser settings.

8. Your Rights

Under Swiss data protection laws and the EU GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access: Request information about the cookies we use and any personal data collected.
  • Rectification: Correct any inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: Request deletion of cookies or related data (via browser settings or by contacting us).
  • Restriction: Limit how we process your data.
  • Objection: Object to the use of non-essential cookies.
  • Data Portability: Request a copy of your data in a structured format (if applicable).

To exercise these rights, please contact us at hello@soyth.ch.

9. Compliance with Swiss and EU Regulations

Soyth complies with the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP) and the EU GDPR. We ensure that:

  • Cookies are only used with a clear legal basis (legitimate interest or consent).
  • Users are informed about cookie usage through this policy and the cookie banner.
  • Consent for non-essential cookies is obtained before they are set.
  • Users can withdraw consent or manage preferences at any time.

For users in other regions, we adhere to applicable local data protection laws to the extent they apply to our services.

10. Contact Us

If you have questions about this Cookie Policy or how we use cookies, please contact us at:

Email: hello@soyth.ch

We aim to respond to all inquiries within 30 days, in line with Swiss and EU data protection requirements.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in our practices or legal requirements. Any updates will be posted on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

We use cookies. Some cookies are essential for this site to function and cannot be turned off. Others are set to help us understand how our service performs and is used, and to support our marketing efforts. Learn more in our Cookie Policy.
